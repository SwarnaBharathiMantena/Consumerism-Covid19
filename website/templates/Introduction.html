Consumerism is the idea that increasing consumption of goods and services purchased in the market is always a desirable goal and that a person's wellbeing and happiness depends fundamentally on obtaining consumer goods and material possessions.

Post-consumerism is a view or ideology that well-being, as distinct from material prominence, is the aim of life, and often suggesting that there is a growing willingness to assert such. Post-consumerism can also be viewed as moving beyond the current model of addictive consumerism.

Through the last several months, corona-virus is spreading relentlessly through the world creating a path of death and destruction – to human life & to the economy. But just like all past pandemics, world wars and depressions, our race will ultimately win this enduring war. At the same time, we will also evolve & reshape their lifestyles adapting to a 'new-normal'.

Isn’t it interesting to know people’s opinions? To know their personal interpretation of the pandemic, what they think could be the new normal, variations (increase/decrease) in their spendings by industry, and what kind of post-consumerist they think they will be!
